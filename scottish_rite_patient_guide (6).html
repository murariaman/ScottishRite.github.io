Dressing for Your X-Ray
Gym clothes? You may be able to skip the gown entirely
0–5 min
✅ Wearing gym clothes?
If you wore a loose, metal-free top, active wear, and no underwire — you may not need a gown at all. The technician will confirm when you arrive.
👕 Need a gown?
If you have metal on your clothing, the technician will take you to a private dressing room. Keep your bag with you — no locker needed.
Check the "What to Wear" tab — wearing the right clothes from home can eliminate this step completely and save several minutes.
📍 HC Dressing Rooms CC.30E–CC.30J · only if needed
📸
X-Ray Exam
Quick and painless — just hold still for a few seconds
~5–10 min
The radiology technician will take your X-ray. It's completely painless — you'll hold still in one or two positions for a few seconds each. Results go directly to your doctor right away.
For scoliosis / EOS scans: the spine is imaged last. The 3D EOS involves a small amount more radiation — ask the technician any questions you have.
📍 X-Ray Rooms CC.205 · CC.222 · CC.235 · CC.249 · CC.250 · EOS CC.34

See Your Doctor
Your doctor reviews your X-ray and discusses next steps
~20–30 min
Staff will bring you from the X-ray room directly to your exam room. Your doctor will have already reviewed your results and will walk you through what they found. If you wore gym clothes and skipped the gown, no change needed — you're already set.
Write down any questions you want to ask beforehand — this is the best time to get answers from your care team.

Part 1 — What to Wear From Home
Do this before you leave — it makes everything faster once you're here

Wear something comfortable and easy to take off — think gym clothes or active wear. You'll be changing into a gown for the X-ray, so the easier your outfit is to remove and put back on, the faster the whole process goes.

✅ Great choices
  • T-shirt or loose top
  • Athletic shorts or joggers
  • Slip-on shoes or sneakers
  • Sports bra (no underwire)
  • Leggings or sweatpants
❌ Avoid these
  • Underwire bra
  • Jeans with metal buttons / zippers
  • Belts with metal buckles
  • Necklaces or chains near chest
  • Tight-fitting clothing
💡 Why it matters: Metal near your chest or back can interfere with the X-ray image and may mean having to redo it. Loose clothing also means you spend less time in the dressing room — getting you to your doctor faster.
🏥
Part 2 — Changing into Your Gown (at the Clinic)
This happens after Radiology check-in, in the HC Dressing Rooms

A technician will take you to one of the private HC Dressing Rooms (CC.30E–CC.30J). You'll be given a clean hospital gown to change into. Here's what to know:

🎒
Keep your bag and clothes with you. No locker needed — carry everything into the dressing room and take it with you throughout.
🔒
The rooms are private. Each HC dressing room has a door that locks. Take your time — there's no rush.
✅
The gown stays on for your doctor's visit. You won't need to get changed again before seeing your doctor — saving a whole extra step.
👕
Remove everything from the waist up including any chest or back jewellery before putting on the gown. Keep bottoms on unless instructed otherwise.
💡 If you wore the right clothes from home (Part 1), this whole step takes about 2–3 minutes.
📍 HC Dressing Rooms CC.30E · CC.30F · CC.30G · CC.30H · CC.30J

Pathway Differences
♀ Female patients
  • Routine lab screening step (Stop 2)
  • Sample collected early — results ready for X-ray
  • No waiting for lab results afterward
ℹ️ Scoliosis scans: spine is imaged last. Ask the technician anything.
♂ Male patients
  • No lab stop needed
  • Go straight to Radiology after check-in
  • Fewer total stops, shorter visit

Day-of Tips
🧴
Remove metal near the scan area
Necklaces, belts, underwires — remove anything near your chest, back, or spine.
❓
Ask questions any time
Any staff member in scrubs can help redirect you or answer questions.
🔵
Tuesdays are "Bones Day"
Specialized spine/bone clinics are often on Tuesdays — the department will be busy.
🛗
Elevators are nearby
A, B, C & D elevator banks are on this floor. Follow signs or ask any staff member.
